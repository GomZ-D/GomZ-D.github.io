Founders Day Fresco

By Diego Gomez

On March 4, 1863, President Abraham Lincoln signed a law creating Idaho Territory from parts of Washington Territory and Dakota Territory, establishing his capital at Lewiston. The original Idaho Territory consisted of most of the areas of present-day Idaho, Montana, and Wyoming, and had a population of less than 17,000. Idaho Territory assumed modern state borders in 1868, and was admitted to the Union as a state in 1890. When Idaho became a territory, the legislature was based in the city of Lewiston. However, Boise was growing due to the discovery of gold, and in 1864 the capital moved to Boise. However, the transfer of the capital to Boise created many problems in the territory, due to Boise's location at the southern end of the Territory. Communication problems between the north and south caused some of the Idaho Territory to be moved to other territories
